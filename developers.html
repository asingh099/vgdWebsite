Aayush Singh

"Project Manager/Game Tester"

Hello, my name is Aayush Singh. Currently, I am 15 years old and just passed 9th grade. On this team, I am designated the team manager and lead game tester. My responsibilities for these roles are to coordinate the team, distribute tasks and due dates, in addition to thoroughly testing the game. In addition to this, I help out wherever possible from documentation portfolio, to coding, to sprites and animations. This is my first year doing TSA, and I have already learnt so much. I have learned a lot about leading a team and the importance of teamwork, as well as time management. TSA pushed me to become a more effective leader. The part I enjoy the most about TSA is seeing our final product come together. From the countless hours we have spent together, seeing all the pieces fit together into a well-rounded videogame is incredibly rewarding, showing a testament of our progress and work. Outside of TSA, I enjoy playing basketball, web development, and meeting new people.

Neel Casula

"Lead Artist"

Hi, my name is Neel Casula. I am 15 years old and just completed 9th grade. On the team, I have contributed by being the lead artist, in addition to the secondary game developer. I have specifically helped my making a majority of the towers, viruses, and level screens, in addition to making the code for interacting with the towers. As this is my first year competing in TSA, I have learnt a lot about things such as time management, researching effectively, and debugging issues. Being a part of TSA has shaped me into a stronger person. My favorite part of TSA is seeing how far we have come, all the changes we have made, and all the new people we have met. Aside from TSA, I enjoy playing tennis, hanging out with friends, and learning new things. I love designing and engineering products, as well as writing code to solve problems.

Ajay Prem

"Lead Developer"

Hello, my name is Ajay Prem and I’m a 15-year-old rising sophomore. On our TSA team, I served as the primary game developer. My role involved learning how to program enemies to appear and move along the track, as well as designing towers with specialized abilities like attacking, slowing, and upgrading. This was my first year participating in TSA, and I gained a wide range of skills, both technical and personal, that have helped me grow. One of my favorite parts of TSA is presenting our final product. It’s incredibly rewarding to see all the hard work come together and feel proud of what we’ve accomplished. Outside of TSA, I enjoy weightlifting, spending time with my family and friends, and exploring topics in STEM.
