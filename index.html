Our Story

At our TSA State Conference, we were incredibly happy when we placed 3rd Place in Software Development. But, we were unaware of the fact that another team from our chapter had placed 2nd. Since this was our first year competing in TSA, we didn’t know about the rule that only 1 team could advance per chapter. We were devastated. To make things worse, we placed 6th in Video Game Design, where only the top 5 advance to the National Conference. We had been so confident and excited, but then at once, everything was taken away from us. We started wondering if we could’ve done more, if we had just worked a little harder, would the outcome have been different? It haunted us like a plague. But then, everything changed two weeks before the early submission deadline when we were told that one of the top 5 video game design teams had dropped out, giving us the opportunity to go to the national conference. We graciously accepted, and worked against the clock after we made the bold decision of scrapping our old game and starting from scratch.

Cyber Siege

It's a tower-defense style game centered around cybersecurity. In the game, players work together to defend their servers from incoming viruses. They start with five lives and lose one each time a virus successfully reaches the end of their defenses. Players can use bandwidth—a form of in-game currency—to buy and upgrade towers that destroy incoming viruses. To reinforce the educational aspect, at the end of each round, players must answer cybersecurity-related questions. If they answer incorrectly, they lose a life.
